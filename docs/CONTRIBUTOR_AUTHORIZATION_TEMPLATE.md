# Contributor Rights and Representative Authorization Template

Use one copy for each person who contributed entrant-owned code, content,
design, media, or documentation to SecHub Academy. This is an internal
preparation template, not an organizer-issued form and not legal advice. Use an
appropriate assignment or employment agreement when actual ownership must be
transferred to an individual or organization.

## Contributor and Project

```text
Project: SecHub Academy
Contributor legal name: [REQUIRED]
Contributor email: [REQUIRED]
Entrant legal name: [REQUIRED]
Entrant type: [INDIVIDUAL / TEAM / ORGANIZATION]
Authorized representative: [REQUIRED FOR TEAM OR ORGANIZATION]
Contribution period: [REQUIRED]
Commits, files, or work contributed: [REQUIRED]
```

## Rights Basis

Select the one basis that is accurate and retain the supporting agreement when
one exists:

- [ ] I am a member of the Team entrant and jointly authorize the Team to submit
  our project through the representative named above.
- [ ] My contribution was created within an employment or contractor
  arrangement under which the Organization entrant owns the applicable rights.
- [ ] I transferred the applicable rights to the entrant under a separate signed
  assignment agreement.
- [ ] Other documented rights basis: ________________________________________

Do not rely on an informal non-exclusive permission when the Official Rules
require the Submission to be solely owned by the entrant.

## Contributor Confirmation

I confirm that:

1. The contribution description above is complete and accurate.
2. My original contribution does not knowingly copy or violate any third-party
   copyright, trademark, patent, trade secret, contract, privacy, publicity, or
   other right.
3. I disclosed every third-party library, API, dataset, font, image, audio file,
   template, code sample, and other material included in my contribution.
4. Any AI-assisted material I contributed was created under an account or terms
   that permitted the use, was reviewed by me, and has been checked for apparent
   third-party copying.
5. I am not subject to an employment, school, client, confidentiality, funding,
   or other agreement that prevents the entrant from submitting and using my
   contribution as described in the OpenAI Build Week Official Rules.
6. I authorize the named representative to include the contribution in the
   SecHub Academy submission and to act for the Team or Organization where
   applicable.
7. I understand that the Official Rules provide a non-exclusive judging license
   and certain promotional rights to the Sponsor and Devpost, including use of
   contributor names, likenesses, voices, and images as stated in those rules.
