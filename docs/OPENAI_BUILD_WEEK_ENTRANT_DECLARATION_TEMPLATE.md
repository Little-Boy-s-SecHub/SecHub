## Declaration

I, the undersigned entrant or duly authorized representative, declare that:

1. I have reviewed the OpenAI Build Week Official Rules and am eligible to enter
   on behalf of the entrant identified above.
2. If the entrant is a Team or Organization, I have been appointed and
   authorized to represent it and submit SecHub Academy on its behalf.
3. The entrant owns the original portions of the submitted project and has the
   authority to submit them. All contributors, assignments, employment rights,
   and team permissions needed for the submission have been identified and
   resolved.
4. The project existed before the submission period. The entrant has accurately
   distinguished the baseline from the meaningful extensions created during the
   submission period in `docs/HACKATHON_SUBMISSION_RECORD.md`.
5. Codex and GPT-5.6 were used as described in the README. The entrant directed,
   reviewed, tested, and adopted the AI-assisted work and does not represent
   unreviewed AI output as independently verified human work.
6. Every retained third-party SDK, API, library, font, image, data source,
   service, container image, and open-source component is used with permission
   and in compliance with its applicable license and terms. The principal items
   are recorded in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`.
7. The entrant has reviewed `docs/IP_AND_ASSET_PROVENANCE.md`, resolved every
   item marked as requiring confirmation, and retained supporting evidence.
8. The separate learning-content repository is owned by the entrant or is used
   under an explicit license or written authorization that permits the submitted
   use.
9. The submitted text, screenshots, diagrams, video, narration, music, logos,
   avatars, and other media do not violate copyright, trademark, patent,
   contract, privacy, publicity, trade-secret, or other third-party rights.
10. The demo video is under three minutes, is publicly visible on YouTube,
    contains clear audio explaining the project and its use of Codex and GPT-5.6,
    and contains no uncleared third-party marks or copyrighted material.
11. The project does not contain viruses, Trojan horses, worms, spyware,
    disabling devices, or code intended to harm real systems. SecHub does include
    intentionally vulnerable training applications and defensive-security
    examples. They are disclosed as training material, use generated flags and
    fictional targets, and are intended to run only in controlled lab runtimes.
12. No secret, private credential, private personal data, or confidential
    third-party material has been committed to the public repository or included
    in submission media.
13. The entrant has not received disqualifying financial or preferential support
    from the Sponsor or Administrator as described in the Official Rules.
14. The working application and any private testing credentials will remain
    available to the Sponsor, Administrator, and judges free of charge and
    without restriction through the end of the judging period.
15. The entrant understands that the submission remains the entrant's
    intellectual property while the Official Rules grant the Sponsor a
    non-exclusive license to use it for judging and grant the Sponsor and Devpost
    the promotional rights described in those rules.
16. Every contributor whose name, likeness, voice, or image appears in the
    submission has been informed of the applicable promotional terms and has
    authorized the representative where required.
17. All required submission materials and testing instructions are in English,
    or include the required English translation.
18. All statements in the Devpost submission, README, demonstration, testing
    instructions, and this declaration are accurate to the best of my knowledge.
